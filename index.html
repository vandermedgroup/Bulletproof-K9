The Belgian Malinois

One breed. Bred right,
trained deliberately.

We work with the Belgian Malinois and nothing else. Our dogs descend from established working lines shaped by the KNPV tradition, the demanding Dutch program that has produced the world's most capable police and protection dogs for generations.

That focus is deliberate. Working lines bring the drive, nerve strength, and clear-headed stability that real capability demands, while our selection prioritizes health and functional prowess so each dog can do the work, and live the life, it's bred for.

Our Training Philosophy

We train the dog
in front of us.

Our approach is built on mutual trust, clear communication, and consistent positive reinforcement.

We understand each dog as an individual, tailoring training to their unique temperament, drives, and capabilities. No template, no shortcuts. The result is a dog that is genuinely reliable: precise on command, settled at home, and confident in the work.

Common Questions

Good questions to ask us.

Why only Belgian Malinois?

Focus. The Malinois, bred from proven working lines and the KNPV tradition, offers a rare combination of drive, nerve strength, health, and trainability. Working with one breed lets us know our dogs deeply and match them precisely.

Are your dogs safe around children and family?

Our Family Companion dogs are well socialized and good with children. Temperament is central to how we select and raise every dog. Protection behaviors, where trained, are command-driven, never triggered by ordinary family life.

How does your training approach work?

It's built on mutual trust, clear communication, and consistent positive reinforcement. We treat each dog as an individual and tailor training to their temperament, drives, and capabilities, so the result is genuinely reliable, not just rehearsed.

What does a dog cost?

Because each dog and program is individual, pricing is shared by private consultation once we understand what you need. Reach out to Chris and we'll walk you through options honestly.

Do you work with police departments and professional handlers?
